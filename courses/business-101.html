Business 101: Introduction to Business

Course Code: BUS 101
Credits: 3
Prerequisites: None

Course Description

This course provides a comprehensive introduction to the world of business. Students will explore fundamental concepts in business management, economics, marketing, finance, and entrepreneurship. The course is designed to give students a solid foundation for further study in business or for those seeking to understand how businesses operate in today's economy.

Learning Objectives

  • Understand the basic principles of business management and organization
  • Explore different business structures and their legal implications
  • Learn fundamental concepts of marketing and customer relations
  • Examine basic financial principles and accounting practices
  • Study entrepreneurship and small business development
  • Analyze ethical considerations in business decision-making

Topics Covered

  • Introduction to Business and Economic Systems
  • Forms of Business Ownership
  • Management and Leadership
  • Marketing Principles
  • Financial Management
  • Human Resources
  • Business Ethics and Social Responsibility
  • Entrepreneurship and Innovation

Assessment

  • Participation: 10%
  • Quizzes: 20%
  • Midterm Exam: 30%
  • Final Project: 25%
  • Final Exam: 15%
